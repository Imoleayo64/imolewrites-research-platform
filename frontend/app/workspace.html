Adaptive Machine Learning for Climate Modeling

By Imole Ayodele, Dr. Renée Moreau — Department of Environmental Science

Climate modeling sits at the intersection of physics, statistics, and computation. Despite remarkable advances in numerical weather prediction over the past decade, long-horizon projections remain limited by the complexity of coupled Earth-system feedbacks [1].

1.1 Motivation

Traditional ensemble approaches scale poorly with the dimensionality of modern reanalysis datasets. This paper argues that adaptive learning algorithms — those that re-weight features in response to distributional shift — offer a principled path toward more robust regional forecasts.

The central challenge is not a lack of data, but a lack of methods that remain calibrated under non-stationary conditions. — Okafor (2024)

Our contributions are threefold:

  • We formalize a framework for adaptive climate downscaling.
  • We introduce a validation protocol that rewards temporal stability.
  • We demonstrate gains of 18% RMSE reduction across three basins.

The remainder of this manuscript reviews related work, describes our methodology, and reports empirical results across heterogeneous climate regimes.
